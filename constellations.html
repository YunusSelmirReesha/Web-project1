Magic of Constellations

Ursa Major

Ursa Major, Latin for "Greater Bear," is one of the most prominent and easily recognizable constellations in the northern sky. Known for its distinctive shape, which resembles a bear with a long tail, it has captivated stargazers for millennia. Home to the famous Big Dipper, Ursa Major has been a symbol of navigation and myth.

Orion the Hunter

Orion is one of the most iconic constellations, easily recognizable and beloved by stargazers around the world. Its brilliance and distinct shape make it a beacon during winter nights in the Northern Hemisphere. Known as "The Hunter," it inspires mythology and astronomy alike.
